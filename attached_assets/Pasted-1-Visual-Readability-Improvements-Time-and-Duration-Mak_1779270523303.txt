1. Visual & Readability Improvements
Time and Duration
Make the duration (e.g., 90 min) visually more distinct by slightly increasing font size or bolding it next to the clock icon.
Activity Titles
Slightly enlarge or accent the activity titles (e.g., “Entry Ticket to Chateau de Beaufort”) so they stand out from the “Things To Do” list.
Things To Do
Make long descriptions collapsible with a “Read more” toggle.
Ensure the bulleted list is neatly spaced and visually separated from the title.
Breaks (Lunch/Coffee)
Use a different background shade or subtle border for breaks.
Slightly enlarge the icon (fork/coffee) for instant recognition.
Travel Time
Add a subtle connector line or arrow between activities to represent travel.
Bold or color the travel time (e.g., 15 min) for visibility.
Pricing & Availability
Highlight price with a colored badge (e.g., €8).
Keep availability text subtle but legible.
Spacing & Hierarchy
Add a bit more spacing between sections and optionally shade alternating activities lightly.
Interactive Links
Make links (e.g., “Find restaurants”) more visually distinct, bright orange and underlined.
2. Location & Travel Enhancements
Departure & Arrival Locations
Show a small location pin icon for each activity.
Tooltip/hover can display full address or coordinates.

Example:

📍 Chateau de Beaufort, Luxembourg
Travel Time Analysis
Calculate travel time from return location of current activity to departure location of next activity using stored coordinates.

Display travel time inline, e.g.:

🚗 15 min from Chateau de Beaufort → Lunch near Luxembourg
Nearby Amenities
If possible, calculate distance to nearby restaurants or coffee shops.

Show inline suggestion for convenience:

🍴 5 min walk to nearest restaurant
Integration in Timeline

Add location and travel info in a subtle inline layout:

11:30 – Lunch break 🍴
📍 Near Luxembourg City
🚶 10 min from Chateau de Beaufort
Goal
Users should instantly understand:
Where they need to be next
How long each activity takes
Travel time and nearby options
Timeline should remain clean, visually consistent, and easy to scan.
